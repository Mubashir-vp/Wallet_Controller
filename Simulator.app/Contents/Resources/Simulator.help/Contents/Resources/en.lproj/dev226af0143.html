Interact with tvOS

Use the keyboard, a simulated or physical Apple TV remote, or an MFi controller to interact with the focus-based user interface of a simulated tvOS device.

Use the keyboard

  • Use the keyboard on the Mac to simulate a Bluetooth keyboard attached to the Apple TV.

    Move the focus to the left: Press the Left Arrow key.

    Move the focus to the right: Press the Right Arrow key.

    Move the focus up: Press the Up Arrow key.

    Move the focus down down: Press the Down Arrow key.

    Trigger the action for the current focus: Press the Return key.

    Move up one level in the navigation hierarchy: Press the Escape key.

Use a simulated Apple TV Remote

  • Choose Hardware > Show Apple TV Remote.

Simulate using the touch surface by using the pointer along with the Option key:

  • Navigate by holding down the Option key while moving the pointer over the touch surface.

  • Select a focus item by Option-clicking.

  • Open the contextual menu by Option-clicking and holding.

The three buttons on the bottom area of the remote simulate other buttons on the remote:

Menu button: Option-click to move up one level in the navigation hierarchy.

TV button: Option-click to return to the main TV menu. Option-click and hold to put the simulated Apple TV into sleep mode.

Play/Pause button: Option-click to play and pause, and to trigger the action of the current focus item.

Siri button: Activate Siri.

Volume buttons: Simulate the volume up and down buttons.

Use a physical remote or MFi controller

  1. Unpair the remote or controller if it is already paired with a device.

  2. Turn off the remote or controller.

  3. On the Mac, Open Bluetooth in System Preferences.

  4. In Bluetooth preferences, turn Bluetooth on if it is off.

  5. Turn on the remote or controller.

    Some remotes or controllers may require turning on a pairing mode. For information on a pairing mode for your device, see the documentation for your device. To enter pairing mode on on an Apple TV remote, hold down the Menu and Volume Up (+) buttons for 5 seconds.

  6. In Bluetooth preferences, wait for the remote to appear in the Devices list and then click the Pair button next to the device to pair the remote with the Mac.

    The remote is now ready to be used with Simulator.

Control

Action

Left joystick

Moves the focus to an eligible view in the direction the joystick is moved.

D-Pad

Moves the focus to an eligible view in the direction of the arrow press.

Button A

Trigger the action associated with the current element.

Button B

Move up one level in the navigation hierarchy.
