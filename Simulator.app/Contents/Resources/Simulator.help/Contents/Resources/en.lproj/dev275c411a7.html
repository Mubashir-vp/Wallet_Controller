Interact with watchOS

Simulate gesture

Use the pointer, keyboard, and menu items to make gestures on a simulated iOS device.

Gesture

To Simulate

Tap.

Click.

Double-tap.

Double-click.

Touch and hold.

Click and hold.

Shallow press.

Choose Hardware > Touch Pressure > Shallow Press. Then, click.

Deep press.

Choose Hardware > Touch Pressure > Deep Press. Then, click.

Shallow or deep presses.

On a Mac with a Force Touch Trackpad, choose Hardware > Touch Pressure > Use Trackpad Force. Then, click with the desired pressure.

Two-finger gestures.

Press Option to display touch points.

Move the pointer while pressing the Option key to change the distance between the touch points.

Move the pointer and hold the Shift and Option keys to reposition the touch points.

Two-finger drag.

Click and drag while pressing the Shift and Option keys.

Two-finger pinch, zoom, or rotate.

Click and drag while pressing the Option key.

Activate Siri.

Choose Hardware > Siri.

Simulate the device buttons

Use a combination of the buttons on the bezel of a simulator to and menu items to use a button or switch.

Button

To simulate

Push in the Digital Crown.

Choose Hardware > Home.

Click the Digital Crown on the device bezel.

Rotate the Digital Crown clockwise.

Place the pointer over the Digital Crown on the device bezel and scroll up using a mouse wheel or trackpad.

If the bezel is hidden, move the pointer over the simulator window, then scroll up using a mouse wheel or trackpad.

Rotate the Digital Crown counter-clockwise.

Place the pointer over the Digital Crown on the device bezel and scroll down using a mouse wheel or trackpad.

If the bezel is hidden, move the pointer over the simulator window, then scroll down using a mouse wheel or trackpad.

Push the Side button.

Choose Hardware > Side Button.

Click the Side button on the device bezel.

Ring/Silent

Click the Ring/Silent button on the device bezel.
