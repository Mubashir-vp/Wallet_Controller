Add a complication to the gallery of the Watch App

In watchOS 3 and later, users can configure multiple watch faces in the gallery area of the Apple Watch app on iPhone. To add your complication to the gallery, you create a complication bundle that gives the user a preview of your complication. The complication bundle is stored in your iOS app bundle and used by the Apple Watch app to populate the gallery area. Each complication bundle includes localizable, static previews of the complications that you build using the ClockKit framework. Follow the steps below to create your complication bundle and add it to your iPhone app.

  1. In your Complications controller code, implement getLocalizableSampleTemplateForComplication:withHandler:. In your implementation, build the templates by using text or image provider calls.

    Important: The keys that you use in the text provider calls should appear in the localized strings files named ckcomplication.strings, and the strings files should also be in your WatchKit extension target.

  2. Launch your iOS app in Simulator and make sure that your app is frontmost on that simulated device.

  3. Select the window for the watchOS device that is paired with the iPhone.

  4. Choose File > Save Complication Bundle.

    Name the complication bundle and save it in a temporary location.

  5. Add the generated complication bundle to your iPhone app.

    Important: You must add the complication bundle as a resource to the top level of your iPhone app—don't add it to your watchOS app or extension. When you add the bundle, be sure to select “Create folder references” in the “Choose options for adding these files” Xcode dialog.

You can confirm that the complication bundle was correctly added to your app by building and launching your app on iPhone, and then launching the Apple Watch app and looking for an entry for your app in the gallery area.
